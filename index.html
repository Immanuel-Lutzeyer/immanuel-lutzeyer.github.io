Leadership for organizational adaptation.

My central question: How can leadership become a distributed organizational capability rather than the work of a few people at the top?

Research agenda

Adaptation as a collective accomplishment.

Organizations do not adapt through executive decisions alone. Strategic direction must be interpreted, translated, and enacted across levels. My research brings strategic leadership and organizational behavior into conversation to explain that collective process.

Drawing on dynamic capabilities, strategic leadership, and sociomaterial perspectives, I examine how managerial cognition, knowledge integration, and coordinated action enable—or constrain—organizational adaptation.

01 · Levels

Leadership beyond the apex

How upper-level and middle managers contribute distinct but interdependent forms of strategic leadership.

02 · Mechanisms

Cognition into collective action

How shared cognition, knowledge integration, and coordination become microfoundations of dynamic capabilities.

03 · Contexts

Change that rewrites work

How persistent crisis, artificial intelligence, and hybrid work reshape managerial roles, decisions, and coordination.

Teaching

Rigor that students can use.

I teach independently at Bachelor’s and Master’s level. My courses combine research literacy with collaborative, experience-based formats so that students learn to evaluate evidence, work through ambiguity, and make defensible managerial judgments.

Teaching approach

I treat management education as guided inquiry. Students work with theory, empirical evidence, and structured experiences—not to memorize a preferred answer, but to understand mechanisms, boundary conditions, and trade-offs.

Academic profile

Psychological foundations. Organizational questions.

My background in economic, organizational, and social psychology informs how I approach strategy: organizational capabilities ultimately depend on how people perceive change, integrate knowledge, and coordinate action.

Since 2021, I have been a PhD candidate and research associate at the Chair of Work, Human Resources and Leadership at Ruhr University Bochum. My dissertation examines how organizations manage adaptation under dynamic technological and environmental change.

My doctoral work includes an international research collaboration with Connie Zheng at the University of Adelaide.
